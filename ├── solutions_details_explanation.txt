Q.1: Top 10 Over-70-Year-Old Clients with Biggest Internet Sales
Purpose: Identify the top 10 customers over 70 years old with the highest total internet sales, displaying their name, age, phone number, email address, and total sales amount.

Query:

sql

Copy
SELECT TOP 10 C.FirstName + ' ' + C.LastName AS Customer_Name, C.AddressLine1, 
DATEDIFF(YEAR, CONVERT(DATE, BirthDate), GETDATE()) AS Age, C.Phone, C.EmailAddress,
ROUND(SUM(FIS.SalesAmount), 2) AS Total_Sales
FROM dbo.DimCustomer C
JOIN dbo.FactInternetSales FIS ON C.CustomerKey = FIS.CustomerKey
WHERE DATEDIFF(YEAR, CONVERT(DATE, BirthDate), GETDATE()) > 70
GROUP BY C.CustomerKey, C.FirstName, C.LastName, C.BirthDate, C.AddressLine1, C.Phone, C.EmailAddress
ORDER BY Total_Sales DESC
Explanation:

Tables Involved:
DimCustomer (C): Contains customer information (e.g., FirstName, LastName, BirthDate, AddressLine1, Phone, EmailAddress, CustomerKey).
FactInternetSales (FIS): Contains internet sales transactions with fields like CustomerKey and SalesAmount.
Key Components:
Concatenation: C.FirstName + ' ' + C.LastName AS Customer_Name combines the first and last names into a single column.
Age Calculation: DATEDIFF(YEAR, CONVERT(DATE, BirthDate), GETDATE()) calculates the customer’s age by finding the year difference between BirthDate and the current date (GETDATE()). The CONVERT(DATE, BirthDate) ensures the BirthDate is in a proper date format.
Aggregation: ROUND(SUM(FIS.SalesAmount), 2) sums the SalesAmount for each customer and rounds to two decimal places.
Filtering: WHERE DATEDIFF(YEAR, CONVERT(DATE, BirthDate), GETDATE()) > 70 restricts the results to customers over 70 years old.
Grouping: GROUP BY C.CustomerKey, C.FirstName, C.LastName, C.BirthDate, C.AddressLine1, C.Phone, C.EmailAddress ensures aggregation by unique customers and includes all non-aggregated columns.
Limiting: TOP 10 restricts the output to the top 10 customers.
Sorting: ORDER BY Total_Sales DESC sorts results by total sales in descending order.
Logic Flow:
Join DimCustomer and FactInternetSales on CustomerKey to link customers with their internet sales.
Filter for customers over 70 using the WHERE clause.
Group by customer details to calculate total sales per customer.
Select the top 10 based on total sales, displaying the required fields.
Output:
Columns: Customer_Name, AddressLine1, Age, Phone, EmailAddress, Total_Sales.
Example: "John Doe, 123 Main St, 72, 555-1234, john.doe@email.com, 15000.50".
Notes:
The DATEDIFF(YEAR, ...) function may slightly overestimate age if the customer’s birthday hasn’t occurred this year. For precision, a more complex age calculation could be used.
Including CustomerKey in the GROUP BY ensures uniqueness, even if names are duplicated.
Q.2: Total Sales for Each Product Category (Internet and Reseller)
Purpose: Calculate the total sales (combining Internet and Reseller sales) for each product category, displaying the product category key, name, and total sales amount.

Query:

sql

Copy
SELECT PC.ProductCategoryKey, PC.EnglishProductCategoryName, ROUND(SUM(SM.SalesAmount), 2) Total_Sales
FROM dbo.DimProduct DP
JOIN (
    SELECT ProductKey, SalesAmount
    FROM dbo.FactResellerSales
    UNION ALL
    SELECT ProductKey, SalesAmount
    FROM dbo.FactInternetSales
) SM ON SM.ProductKey = DP.ProductKey
JOIN dbo.DimProductSubcategory PS ON PS.ProductSubcategoryKey = DP.ProductSubcategoryKey
JOIN dbo.DimProductCategory PC ON PC.ProductCategoryKey = PS.ProductCategoryKey
GROUP BY PC.ProductCategoryKey, PC.EnglishProductCategoryName
ORDER BY Total_Sales DESC
Explanation:

Tables Involved:
DimProduct (DP): Contains product details (ProductKey, ProductSubcategoryKey).
FactResellerSales: Contains reseller sales data (ProductKey, SalesAmount).
FactInternetSales: Contains internet sales data (ProductKey, SalesAmount).
DimProductSubcategory (PS): Links products to subcategories (ProductSubcategoryKey, ProductCategoryKey).
DimProductCategory (PC): Contains category details (ProductCategoryKey, EnglishProductCategoryName).
Key Components:
Union:
sql

Copy
SELECT ProductKey, SalesAmount FROM dbo.FactResellerSales
UNION ALL
SELECT ProductKey, SalesAmount FROM dbo.FactInternetSales
Combines sales from both FactResellerSales and FactInternetSales into a single dataset (SM), including only ProductKey and SalesAmount. UNION ALL is used for performance, as it doesn’t remove duplicates (assumed unnecessary here).
Joins:
SM.ProductKey = DP.ProductKey: Links combined sales to products.
PS.ProductSubcategoryKey = DP.ProductSubcategoryKey: Links products to subcategories.
PC.ProductCategoryKey = PS.ProductCategoryKey: Links subcategories to categories.
Aggregation: ROUND(SUM(SM.SalesAmount), 2) calculates total sales per category, rounded to two decimal places.
Grouping: GROUP BY PC.ProductCategoryKey, PC.EnglishProductCategoryName groups results by category.
Sorting: ORDER BY Total_Sales DESC sorts by total sales in descending order.
Logic Flow:
Combine internet and reseller sales using UNION ALL.
Join the combined sales with DimProduct, DimProductSubcategory, and DimProductCategory to map sales to product categories.
Aggregate sales by category and display the results.
Output:
Columns: ProductCategoryKey, EnglishProductCategoryName, Total_Sales.
Example: "1, Bikes, 500000.75".
Notes:
The UNION ALL assumes no duplicate sales records across the two tables for the same ProductKey. If duplicates exist, UNION might be needed, but it’s slower.
Including ProductCategoryKey in the GROUP BY ensures uniqueness, even if category names are duplicated.
Q.3: Total Sales Amount for Each Month in 2013
Purpose: Calculate the total sales amount (Internet and Reseller) for each month in 2013, displaying the year, month number, month name, and total sales.

Query:

sql

Copy
SELECT D.CalendarYear, D.MonthNumberOfYear, D.EnglishMonthName, ROUND(SUM(S.SalesAmount), 2) Total_Sales
FROM dbo.DimDate D 
JOIN (
    SELECT OrderDateKey, SalesAmount
    FROM dbo.FactResellerSales
    UNION ALL
    SELECT OrderDateKey, SalesAmount
    FROM dbo.FactInternetSales
) S ON S.OrderDateKey = D.DateKey
WHERE D.CalendarYear = '2013'
GROUP BY D.CalendarYear, D.MonthNumberOfYear, D.EnglishMonthName
ORDER BY 2
Explanation:

Tables Involved:
DimDate (D): Contains date details (DateKey, CalendarYear, MonthNumberOfYear, EnglishMonthName).
FactResellerSales: Contains reseller sales (OrderDateKey, SalesAmount).
FactInternetSales: Contains internet sales (OrderDateKey, SalesAmount).
Key Components:
Union:
sql

Copy
SELECT OrderDateKey, SalesAmount FROM dbo.FactResellerSales
UNION ALL
SELECT OrderDateKey, SalesAmount FROM dbo.FactInternetSales
Combines sales from both tables into a single dataset (S).
Join: S.OrderDateKey = D.DateKey links sales to the date dimension.
Filtering: WHERE D.CalendarYear = '2013' restricts results to 2013.
Aggregation: ROUND(SUM(S.SalesAmount), 2) calculates total sales per month.
Grouping: GROUP BY D.CalendarYear, D.MonthNumberOfYear, D.EnglishMonthName groups by year and month.
Sorting: ORDER BY 2 sorts by MonthNumberOfYear (column 2) for chronological order.
Logic Flow:
Combine sales data from both sources.
Join with DimDate to get month and year details.
Filter for 2013 and aggregate sales by month.
Display results in chronological order.
Output:
Columns: CalendarYear, MonthNumberOfYear, EnglishMonthName, Total_Sales.
Example: "2013, 1, January, 10000.25".
Notes:
The query assumes OrderDateKey aligns with DateKey. If date formats differ, additional conversion may be needed.
Sorting by MonthNumberOfYear ensures months appear in calendar order (1–12).
Q.4: Top-Performing Sales Region in Each Country
Purpose: Identify the top-performing sales region in each country based on total sales (Internet and Reseller), displaying the country, region, and total sales amount.

Query:

sql

Copy
WITH CTE AS (
    SELECT T.SalesTerritoryCountry, T.SalesTerritoryRegion, T.Total_Sales,
    RANK() OVER(PARTITION BY T.SalesTerritoryCountry ORDER BY T.Total_Sales DESC) AS RANK
    FROM (
        SELECT ST.SalesTerritoryRegion, ST.SalesTerritoryCountry, ROUND(SUM(S.SalesAmount), 2) Total_Sales
        FROM dbo.DimSalesTerritory ST
        JOIN (
            SELECT SalesTerritoryKey, SalesAmount
            FROM dbo.FactResellerSales
            UNION ALL
            SELECT SalesTerritoryKey, SalesAmount
            FROM dbo.FactInternetSales
        ) S ON S.SalesTerritoryKey = ST.SalesTerritoryKey
        GROUP BY ST.SalesTerritoryRegion, ST.SalesTerritoryCountry
    ) T
)
SELECT SalesTerritoryCountry, SalesTerritoryRegion, Total_Sales
FROM CTE 
WHERE RANK = 1
ORDER BY Total_Sales DESC
Explanation:

Tables Involved:
DimSalesTerritory (ST): Contains territory details (SalesTerritoryKey, SalesTerritoryCountry, SalesTerritoryRegion).
FactResellerSales: Contains reseller sales (SalesTerritoryKey, SalesAmount).
FactInternetSales: Contains internet sales (SalesTerritoryKey, SalesAmount).
Key Components:
Inner Subquery:
sql

Copy
SELECT ST.SalesTerritoryRegion, ST.SalesTerritoryCountry, ROUND(SUM(S.SalesAmount), 2) Total_Sales
FROM dbo.DimSalesTerritory ST
JOIN (
    SELECT SalesTerritoryKey, SalesAmount FROM dbo.FactResellerSales
    UNION ALL
    SELECT SalesTerritoryKey, SalesAmount FROM dbo.FactInternetSales
) S ON S.SalesTerritoryKey = ST.SalesTerritoryKey
GROUP BY ST.SalesTerritoryRegion, ST.SalesTerritoryCountry
Combines sales, joins with DimSalesTerritory, and calculates total sales per region and country.
CTE:
sql

Copy
WITH CTE AS (
    SELECT T.SalesTerritoryCountry, T.SalesTerritoryRegion, T.Total_Sales,
    RANK() OVER(PARTITION BY T.SalesTerritoryCountry ORDER BY T.Total_Sales DESC) AS RANK
    FROM (...)
)
Assigns a rank to each region within a country based on Total_Sales, with RANK() = 1 indicating the top region.
Filtering: WHERE RANK = 1 selects only the top-ranked region per country.
Sorting: ORDER BY Total_Sales DESC sorts results by total sales.
Logic Flow:
Combine sales data and aggregate by region and country.
Use a CTE to rank regions within each country.
Select the top region (RANK = 1) for each country.
Output:
Columns: SalesTerritoryCountry, SalesTerritoryRegion, Total_Sales.
Example: "United States, Northwest, 200000.00".
Notes:
If multiple regions tie for the top sales in a country, RANK() will assign them the same rank, and all will appear in the output. Use ROW_NUMBER() for a single result per country.
The query assumes valid SalesTerritoryKey mappings.
Q.5: Top 2 Product Categories by Quantity Sold
Purpose: Identify the top 2 product categories with the highest quantity sold (Internet and Reseller), displaying the category name and total order quantity.

Query:

sql

Copy
SELECT TOP 2 PC.EnglishProductCategoryName, SUM(OrderQuantity) AS Total_OrderQuantity
FROM dbo.DimProduct DP
JOIN (
    SELECT OrderDateKey, ProductKey, OrderQuantity, SalesAmount
    FROM dbo.FactResellerSales
    UNION ALL
    SELECT OrderDateKey, ProductKey, OrderQuantity, SalesAmount
    FROM dbo.FactInternetSales
) SM ON SM.ProductKey = DP.ProductKey
JOIN dbo.DimProductSubcategory PS ON PS.ProductSubcategoryKey = DP.ProductSubcategoryKey
JOIN dbo.DimProductCategory PC ON PC.ProductCategoryKey = PS.ProductCategoryKey
GROUP BY PC.ProductCategoryKey, PC.EnglishProductCategoryName
ORDER BY Total_OrderQuantity DESC
Explanation:

Tables Involved:
Same as Q.2, with the addition of OrderQuantity from FactResellerSales and FactInternetSales.
Key Components:
Union: Combines OrderDateKey, ProductKey, OrderQuantity, and SalesAmount from both sales tables.
Joins: Links sales to products, subcategories, and categories.
Aggregation: SUM(OrderQuantity) calculates the total quantity sold per category.
Limiting: TOP 2 restricts to the top 2 categories.
Grouping: GROUP BY PC.ProductCategoryKey, PC.EnglishProductCategoryName groups by category.
Sorting: ORDER BY Total_OrderQuantity DESC sorts by quantity.
Logic Flow:
Combine sales data, including quantities.
Map to product categories via joins.
Aggregate quantities by category and select the top 2.
Output:
Columns: EnglishProductCategoryName, Total_OrderQuantity.
Example: "Bikes, 5000".
Notes:
The query focuses on OrderQuantity, not SalesAmount, unlike Q.2.
Ties in quantity are resolved arbitrarily unless TOP 2 WITH TIES is used.

Q.7: Year-over-Year Sales Growth for 2013 vs. 2012
Purpose: Calculate the year-over-year (YoY) sales growth percentage for 2013 compared to 2012, combining Internet and Reseller sales.

Query:

sql

Copy
WITH CTE AS (
    SELECT 
    ROUND(SUM(CASE WHEN D.CalendarYear = '2013' THEN T.SalesAmount ELSE 0 END), 2) AS Sales_2013,
    ROUND(SUM(CASE WHEN D.CalendarYear = '2012' THEN T.SalesAmount ELSE 0 END), 2) AS Sales_2012
    FROM (
        SELECT OrderDateKey, SalesAmount
        FROM dbo.FactResellerSales
        UNION ALL
        SELECT OrderDateKey, SalesAmount
        FROM dbo.FactInternetSales
    ) T
    JOIN dbo.DimDate D ON D.DateKey = T.OrderDateKey
)
SELECT Sales_2013, Sales_2012, ROUND((Sales_2013 - Sales_2012) / Sales_2012 * 100, 2) AS YoYGrowthPercentage
FROM CTE
Explanation:

Tables Involved:
FactResellerSales, FactInternetSales, DimDate.
Key Components:
Union: Combines sales from both tables.
CTE:
sql

Copy
ROUND(SUM(CASE WHEN D.CalendarYear = '2013' THEN T.SalesAmount ELSE 0 END), 2) AS Sales_2013
Uses conditional aggregation to sum sales for 2013 and 2012 separately.
Calculation: (Sales_2013 - Sales_2012) / Sales_2012 * 100 computes the YoY growth percentage.
Logic Flow:
Combine sales and join with DimDate.
Aggregate sales for 2012 and 2013 in a CTE.
Calculate and display the growth percentage.
Output:
Columns: Sales_2013, Sales_2012, YoYGrowthPercentage.
Example: "150000.00, 100000.00, 50.00".
Notes:
Assumes non-zero sales in 2012 to avoid division by zero.
The query doesn’t filter explicitly for 2012/2013, relying on the CASE statements.
Q.8: Customers with Highest Internet Orders and Sales per Year
Purpose: Identify the customer with the highest number of internet orders (and sales as a tiebreaker) for each year, displaying year, customer name, address, phone, email, total quantity, and total sales, for customers with more than 5 orders.

Query:

sql

Copy
WITH CTE AS (
    SELECT CalendarYear, Customer_Name, AddressLine1, Phone, EmailAddress, Total_Qty, Total_Sales,
    RANK() OVER(PARTITION BY CalendarYear ORDER BY Total_Qty DESC, Total_Sales DESC) AS RNK
    FROM (
        SELECT D.CalendarYear, C.FirstName + ' ' + C.LastName AS Customer_Name, C.AddressLine1, C.Phone, C.EmailAddress,
        SUM(OrderQuantity) AS Total_Qty, ROUND(SUM(S.SalesAmount), 2) AS Total_Sales
        FROM dbo.DimCustomer C
        JOIN dbo.FactInternetSales S ON S.CustomerKey = C.CustomerKey
        JOIN dbo.DimDate D ON D.DateKey = S.OrderDateKey
        GROUP BY D.CalendarYear, C.FirstName, C.LastName, C.BirthDate, C.AddressLine1, C.Phone, C.EmailAddress
        HAVING SUM(OrderQuantity) > 5
    ) T
)
SELECT CalendarYear, Customer_Name, AddressLine1, Phone, EmailAddress, Total_Qty, Total_Sales
FROM CTE
WHERE RNK = 1
Explanation:

Tables Involved:
DimCustomer, FactInternetSales, DimDate.
Key Components:
Inner Query:
sql

Copy
SELECT D.CalendarYear, C.FirstName + ' ' + C.LastName AS Customer_Name, ...
SUM(OrderQuantity) AS Total_Qty, ROUND(SUM(S.SalesAmount), 2) AS Total_Sales
...
HAVING SUM(OrderQuantity) > 5
Aggregates orders and sales by customer and year, filtering for customers with more than 5 orders.
CTE:
sql

Copy
RANK() OVER(PARTITION BY CalendarYear ORDER BY Total_Qty DESC, Total_Sales DESC)
Ranks customers within each year, prioritizing higher Total_Qty, with Total_Sales as a tiebreaker.
Filtering: WHERE RNK = 1 selects the top customer per year.
Logic Flow:
Aggregate internet sales by customer and year, filtering for >5 orders.
Rank customers by quantity and sales.
Select the top customer per year.
Output:
Columns: CalendarYear, Customer_Name, AddressLine1, Phone, EmailAddress, Total_Qty, Total_Sales.
Example: "2013, Jane Smith, 456 Oak St, 555-5678, jane@email.com, 100, 5000.00".
Notes:
The HAVING clause ensures only significant customers are considered.
Ties in both quantity and sales may result in multiple top customers per year.
General Observations:
Common Techniques:
UNION ALL is used frequently to combine Internet and Reseller sales, assuming no duplicates.
Joins with dimension tables (DimProduct, DimDate, etc.) map sales to meaningful attributes.
Aggregation (SUM, ROUND) and grouping are central to summarizing data.
Ranking functions (RANK, TOP) identify top performers.
Potential Improvements:
Add error handling for edge cases (e.g., zero sales, missing dates).
Use ROW_NUMBER() instead of RANK() for unique results in Q.4 and Q.8 if desired.
Validate date formats and key alignments across tables.
Performance Considerations:
Indexes on CustomerKey, ProductKey, OrderDateKey, and SalesTerritoryKey would improve join performance.
UNION ALL is efficient, but large datasets may require partitioning or filtering earlier.
